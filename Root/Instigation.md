**Type:** Active / Passive — Psychological Manipulation / Instigator  
**Cost:** Level 2 Spell Slot 
**Range:** Hearing / Conversation Range  
**Duration:** Scene-based / Influence-based

**Description:**  
Instigators specialize in awakening suppressed desires, resentment, and malice hidden within others. Through subtle presence, emotional reading, and carefully chosen words, they manipulate people into escalating conflicts, committing harmful actions, or believing carefully constructed lies — often without realizing they were influenced.

⚠ _“People rarely need to be convinced — only encouraged.”_

### **Passive Effects**

- Sense emotional states, desires, hostility, and hidden intentions
    
- Naturally appear trustworthy, approachable, and persuasive
    
- Gain intuitive understanding of social tension and psychological pressure points
    

### **Active Effects**

- Plant suggestions or impulses that align with the target’s hidden desires
    
- Encourage fights, arguments, reckless decisions, or criminal actions
    
- Strengthen lies and misdirection through psychological reinforcement
    
- Influence targets to escalate existing conflicts or emotional reactions
    

### **Scaling**

**Seq 8 — Emotional Provocation**

- Plant short-term desires or impulses
    
- Increase likelihood of aggressive behavior
    
- Targets may not realize manipulation occurred
    

**Seq 7 — Conflict Catalyst**

- Trigger fights or escalate group tension
    
- Stronger emotional reading and manipulation
    
- Influence multiple individuals in conversation
    

**Seq 6 — Deep Persuasion**

- Plant complex motivations or behavioral tendencies
    
- Strengthen lies significantly
    
- Can manipulate disciplined or trained targets with effort
    

**Seq 5 — Malice Awakening**

- Trigger violent actions or dangerous decisions aligned with target’s inner desires
    
- Influence crowds or multiple targets simultaneously
    
- Extremely difficult for unaware targets to resist
    

### **Limitations**

- Cannot force actions completely against a target’s nature or core beliefs
    
- Strong mental defenses or high willpower reduce effectiveness
    
- Requires social interaction, presence, or psychological connection
